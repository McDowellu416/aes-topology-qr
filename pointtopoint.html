What is a POINT TO POINT Topology?

A point-to-point topology is a direct connection between two points, such as two buildings, two devices or two sites.

It is commonly used where a dedicated link is needed between two specific locations. The link could be wireless, fibre, copper or another communications medium.

Benefits of POINT TO POINT

  • Simple and direct connection between two locations.
  • Can provide dedicated bandwidth for a specific link.
  • Useful for linking buildings, sites or equipment.
  • Can be easier to understand and manage than larger multi-node networks.

Limitations of POINT TO POINT

  • Only connects two points directly.
  • If the link fails, communication between those two points may be lost.
  • Wireless point-to-point links may require line of sight and careful alignment.
  • It is not as flexible as topologies that support many interconnected nodes.
